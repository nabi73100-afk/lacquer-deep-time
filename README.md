# Lacquer Deep-Time Research

This repository develops a theoretical and artistic research framework examining lacquer as a deep-time material system.  
Rather than treating lacquer solely as a craft tradition or aesthetic technique, this project investigates its role as a persistent material medium through which civilizations construct temporal continuity, cultural memory, and technological stability.

Across East Asian archaeological contexts, lacquer frequently survives where organic substrates have disappeared, positioning it as a unique archive of material time and historical endurance.

---

## Conceptual framework

This research approaches lacquer as:

- A deep-time material archive  
- A temporal infrastructure of civilization  
- A medium of historical persistence and cultural continuity  
- A material technology mediating human–nature relations  
- A philosophical substance structuring time perception  

---

## Research directions

- Deep-time aesthetics and material endurance  
- Archaeological persistence of lacquer structures  
- Lacquer and long-duration civilizational systems  
- Material temporality and historical consciousness  
- Cross-media artistic research on deep material memory  
- AI-assisted reconstruction of material time  

---

## Long-term objectives

- Establish lacquer as a key concept in global deep-time humanities  
- Connect archaeology, material science, philosophy, and artistic practice  
- Develop museum-scale research exhibitions on material temporality  
- Construct theoretical models of civilizational material persistence  
- Expand lacquer research into cinematic, literary, and digital domains
